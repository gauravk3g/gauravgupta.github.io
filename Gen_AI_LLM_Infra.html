Understanding How Generative AI Models Are Trained

An Interactive Exploration of LLM Training, Resources, and Real-World Costs

Introduction

When I first started exploring generative AI, what stood out to me was not just how powerful these systems are, but how much effort goes into building them. Models like GPT, Claude, and LLaMA don’t simply “learn overnight.” They are trained on enormous amounts of data using highly advanced infrastructure.

This project is my attempt to break down that complex process into something more approachable. Instead of focusing only on technical jargon, I wanted to show the full journey—from raw data to a working AI system—in a way that feels clear, practical, and meaningful.

Description of the Artifact

This interactive infographic walks through the lifecycle of a large language model. It highlights each major stage in the training pipeline, explains the resources involved, and provides real-world context around cost and scale. The goal is to help bridge the gap between theory and real-world implementation.

Objective

The main goal of this work is to better understand how generative AI models are built and why they require such significant investment. I wanted to explore not just the “how,” but also the “why”—why data matters, why compute is expensive, and why optimization is critical in AI development.

Key Resources and Why They Matter

Thousands of GPUs working in parallel. This is the most expensive part.
⚡ Energy
Training consumes large amounts of electricity, raising sustainability concerns.
⏳ Time

Process / Methodology

To create this artifact, I started by reviewing foundational materials on how large language models work. I then organized the training process into clear stages and connected each stage with its resource requirements.

From there, I focused on presenting the information visually and interactively. My goal was to move beyond a static report and create something that invites exploration.

Tools & Technologies Used

  • ChatGPT for research and structuring ideas
  • HTML, CSS, JavaScript for development
  • Chart.js for data visualization

Value Proposition

This project simplifies a highly complex topic into something that is easier to understand and engage with. It can be useful for students, professionals, and anyone curious about how modern AI systems are built.

Unique Value

What makes this work different is its balance between technical depth and clarity. Instead of overwhelming the reader, it focuses on explaining concepts in a way that feels intuitive and connected to real-world applications.

Relevance to AI/ML Practice

Understanding the training process is essential for anyone working in AI/ML. It helps in making better decisions about model selection, cost optimization, and ethical considerations such as bias and energy consumption.
